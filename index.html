NASA MERRA

MERRA (Modern Era Retrospective-Analysis for Research and Applications) is a reanalysis of global climate observation data for the satellite era. Focusing on the historical analyses of the hydrological cycle on a broad range of weather and climate time scales, MERRA is a project of the NASA Global Modeling and Assimilation Office at NASA Goddard Space Flight Center (GSFC) (Rienecker et al., 2011). MERRA is widely used to study weather and climate variability (Duffy et al, 2012). Three representative MERRA data products with different spatial, temporal, and dimensions are integrated in the current system.

Meteorological Station Measurements

The Global Historical Climatology Network -Daily (GHCN-Daily, Menne et al., 2012) is downloaded from NOAA National Climatic Data Center (http://doi.org/10.7289/V5D21VHZ). The GHCN-Daily dataset contains about 2.5 billion measurements for over 137 variables (such as precipitation, snowfall, maximum and minimum temperature) from more than 100,725 meteorological stations worldwide with the time spanning from 1763 to 2016.
